    Step 6

    Real-life meaning

    For younger people, the story is often about growth, energy, and learning how foods differ. For adults, the same data can support choices around long-term health, weight management, heart disease risk, and diabetes risk.

    Teens

    Focus on growth, steady energy, protein, iron, and vitamin C instead of judging foods by calories alone.

    Young adults

    Compare foods that fit busy schedules while balancing protein, calories, fat, and useful micronutrients.

    Adults

    Compare several measures at once, especially when managing fat, carbs, and calorie density.

    Older adults

    Look for nutrient-dense foods that keep protein and micronutrients visible without letting calories dominate.

    Final takeaway

    Good nutrition is a balancing act.

    The most useful food choices come from comparing multiple factors at once: calories, protein, carbs, fat, and micronutrients all tell part of the story.
